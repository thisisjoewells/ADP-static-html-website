About The Author

Excerpt from the dust jacket: J. Allan Bosworth began writing while still a radioman aboard USS Missouri. World War II had just ended, and the ship was on her long voyage home. A native Californian, he returned to San Francisco and took a job at the Chronicle. Ten years later, having published two novels and a few dozen short stories, he left the newspaper to begin writing on a full time basis.

All the Dark Places - The Motion Picture

Starting in 2010, after directing the short film How To Feel In America Today, we (Vapor) endeavored to adapt All the Dark Places into a motion picture.

  • We began outlining and structure the book for the screen
  • We began concept art
  • We researched and found J. Allan's Bosworth daughters (Mr. Bosworth is deceased)
  • We developed an film option contract
  • We began a business and financing plan
  • We secured a verbal deal, and prepared to mail the contract and option payment,
  • In February 2015 the deal was suddenly called off by the Bosworth daughters for reasons unknown to us

The project is on hiatus indefinitely. If the Bosworth daughters ever change their mind, we sincerely hope they will contact us.  We long for the day when we have the opportunity to collaborate on adapting this great book into a great film.
